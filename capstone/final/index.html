FAST FASHION VS SUSTAINABLE FASHION

Fast fashion focuses on rapidly producing low-cost clothing that mimics current trends. It relies on cheap labor, synthetic materials, and mass production, often at the expense of environmental and human rights. These items are typically worn only a few times before being discarded.

In contrast, sustainable fashion emphasizes ethical labor practices, eco-friendly materials, and long-lasting designs. Brands in this category prioritize transparency, fair wages, low carbon emissions, and minimal waste.

  • Fast Fashion Examples: SHEIN, Zara, Hollister
  • Sustainable Fashion Examples: Patagonia, Everlane, Reformation

Understanding these differences can empower you to make more conscious decisions that align with your values‚Äîand help reduce the harm caused by the fashion industry.
