Annotated Bibliography

Circular Design for Fashion This comprehensive guide explores how design can drive the transition to a circular fashion industry. It emphasizes the importance of designing products with their entire lifecycle in mind, promoting reuse, recycling, and sustainability. The book offers practical strategies for designers and brands to create fashion that aligns with circular economy principles. It's particularly useful for understanding how thoughtful design can reduce waste and extend the life of clothing, aligning well with initiatives focused on upcycling and community-driven exchange.

Second Hand Clothing: A Circular Economy Strategy for Sustainable Consumption This article examines the role of secondhand clothing in promoting sustainable consumption within a circular economy framework. It discusses how extending the lifespan of garments through resale and reuse can significantly reduce environmental impacts. The study provides data on consumer behavior and the environmental benefits of secondhand shopping, making it a valuable resource for understanding the practical implications of circular fashion practices.

The Thrift Paradigm: Reimagining Consumption in a Circular Economy This video explores the cultural and economic aspects of thrift shopping within the context of a circular economy. It highlights how secondhand shopping not only reduces waste but also fosters a sense of community and creativity. The documentary features interviews with consumers and experts, providing diverse perspectives on the benefits and challenges of integrating thrift practices into mainstream fashion consumption. It's an engaging resource that underscores the social dimensions of circular fashion, complementing more technical or design-focused materials.
